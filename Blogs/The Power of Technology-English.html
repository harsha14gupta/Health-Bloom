The Power of Technology

by HARSHA GUPTAnov 15th, 2020

Technology has brought about a massive and welcome change to the healthcare industry. Patients now have access to some of the best diagnostic tools, new and cutting-edge treatments, and a myriad of minimally-invasive procedures resulting in less pain and quicker healing.Remote consultations with specialists, targeted treatments, and the availability of intuitive mobile apps have led to improved patient care and a superior healthcare experience overall. Additionally, the availability of newer treatment technologies leading to better outcomes has enhanced the quality of life of the patients as well.

Digitization of Health Records
Dusty and bulky paper files are slowly giving way to streamlined digital records that are securely uploaded to the cloud and made accessible remotely to patients and healthcare professionals.
 
Mobile App Technology in the Medical Field
Not only do patients have access to quick and accurate medical information using their handheld devices, but they can also use apps to keep track of doctor’s appointments, get reminders to take their medications. Health and fitness apps help people get healthier by tracking their food intake and activity levels and offering customized solutions.

Electronic Medical Records
Electronic medical records or Electronic Health Records (EHRs) consist of digital summaries of a patient’s medical records. They could include diagnoses, lab reports, and details about hospital stays, surgical interventions, and prescriptions. They offer an overview of a patient’s health; permitting a more accurate diagnosis and improved patient care.

Big Data and the Cloud
Big data is a buzzword in different industries, including healthcare. This is because the generation and collection of huge amounts of data from a number of different sources in the healthcare field are now possible. This data is then used for analytics, making predictions about possible epidemics and ultimately preventing deaths.

Telemedicine/Telehealth
Telemedicine takes the digitization of healthcare to its next logical step; letting patients consult with specialist practically anywhere in the world. This is technology applied to the healthcare system to overcome distance barriers and facilitate critical care in emergency situations; potentially saving lives.

With all of this, there is a great potential to use data, analytics and technology to bring about a revolution and advance healthcare innovation for the future.

-> Telemedicine

▪ Market is expected to reach $ 5.4 billion by 2025 growing at a CAGR of 31% from 2019
▪ Though in initial stage, telemedicine adoption has increased since pandemic
▪ Startups such as Practo, M-fine, myUpchar, Tattvan, DocsApp are some providers
▪ Initiatives by Apollo Hospitals in partnership with few states govt are focusing on telehealth

-> Blockchain

▪ Potential to optimize and increase the efficiency of the implemented electronic health records in India
▪ An initiative by NITI Aayog is leveraging blockchain technology for a unified data system in the pharmaceutical drugs supply chain

-> Internet of Things

▪ IoT is swiftly transforming the healthcare industry and has become a key focus area for many start-ups (MedTech and Bio Tech)
▪ IoT is currently used by hospitals to cut costs, and insurance companies to reduce claim payments
▪ Start-ups such as Janitri Innovations and EzeRx developed IoT-enabled devices to monitor the health-rate and conduct tests

Developed nations have been able to harness technology more efficiently for improved patient care; however, developing nations are catching up quickly. Healthcare providers who haven’t yet adopted the tools technology places at their disposal are realizing their vast untapped potential and are making the changes and the investments required to streamline processes, lower costs, increase efficiency and most importantly, to improve quality of care.
